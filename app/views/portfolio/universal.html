Overview

Universal Jobmatch (UJ) a job search, application and matching website, was introduced in November 2012. The current live service is provided by Monster Worldwide Ltd. on behalf of DWP. The contract to provide the service with Monster ends on 18th March 2016. UJ is endeavoring to identify the future service requirements based on prioritised user needs and exploring options for transforming service delivery.

Customer impact

It is a popular service widely used by jobseekers with 4.5 million searches daily. It therefore supports the strategic aim of getting people back into work and is a key tool for job centres.

Risks & dependencies

  • Other teams within DWP are undertaking similar projects and despite current efforts to untangle those functions that overlap with UJ, the major risk for the programme is that its proposals at the end of the process will have already been developed.
  • Successful re-negotiation and extension of the contract with the current service provider is required in order to provide an interim service and adequate time to construct a longer-term solution.
  • Dependent on Universal Credit for the creation of a re-usable service that can be applied to citizens currently on JSA.
